Dōgen Zenji

Dōgen Zenji (1200–1253) was a Japanese Buddhist monk, philosopher, and poet who founded the Sōtō school of Zen in Japan. After studying in China, he returned with a strong emphasis on zazen (seated meditation) as the core of Zen practice. His most famous work, the Shōbōgenzō, offers profound insights into time, being, and enlightenment, blending rigorous philosophy with spiritual depth. Dōgen's teachings continue to influence Zen practitioners around the world today.
